Technical Manual & Hints

1. Turf Interaction

The relationship between landing and travel depends on Firmness and Friction.

  • Turf Friction: Multiplier for forward velocity.
    • 0.85+ (Concrete): Rollout can exceed 100yd on low shots.
    • 0.70 - 0.75 (Firm/Links): Dry summer fairways.
    • 0.60 - 0.65 (Average): Standard recreational fairway.
    • 0.45 - 0.55 (Soft/Wet): Grass "grabs" the ball quickly.
  • Turf Firmness: Controls vertical bounce.
    • 0.30 - 0.40 (Normal): Standard bounce height.
    • 0.15 - 0.25 (Soft): Ball "thuds" with little kick.

2. The "Stinger" Effect

Apex dictates the Descent Angle.

  • Low Apex (< 20yd): Shallow descent. Ball skips and runs because momentum isn't converted into a vertical stop.
  • High Apex (> 30yd): Steep descent. Ball "drops from the sky" and stops fast.

3. Atmospheric Calibration

Hardcoded for Asheville, NC (2,165'):

  • Air Density (ρ): 1.15 kg/m³. Thinner air = less drag/lift.
  • Gravity (g): 9.796 m/s². Slightly weaker than standard 9.81.

Physics Engine Architecture

1. Initial Launch Vector

User inputs are converted into a starting velocity vector in meters per second (m/s):

V₀ = (ClubSpeed_mph × 0.44704) × SmashFactor
Vx = V₀ × cos(LaunchAngle_rad)
Vy = V₀ × sin(LaunchAngle_rad)

2. Aero Flight (Drag & Lift)

Recalculated every 0.015s step. Accounts for wind speed relative to ball velocity.

  • Drag Force: Fd = 0.5 × ρ × V_air² × Cd × Area
  • Lift (Magnus): Fl = 0.5 × ρ × V_air² × Cl × Area
  • Spin Scaling: Cl is dynamically scaled by (Spin / 3000).

3. Integration Step

Force accumulation and position updates:

Ax = (A_drag_x + A_lift_x)
Ay = (-g + A_drag_y + A_lift_y)
V_new = V_old + (A × dt)
P_new = P_old + (V × dt)

4. Ground Game

Triggered when y ≤ 0.

  • The Bounce: If Vy < -1.5, Vy = -Vy × Firmness and Vx = Vx × Friction.
  • The Roll: Horizontal deceleration = g × (1 - Friction).
